# Product-Market Fit (PMF)

A diagnostic framework for evaluating whether a product satisfies a strong market demand. Product-market fit is the inflection point where a startup shifts from searching to scaling. Before PMF, the priority is learning and iterating; after PMF, the priority is growth and efficiency. Misdiagnosing PMF — scaling too early or iterating too long — is one of the most expensive mistakes a product team can make.

## Steps

1. **Define the Target Segment** — Narrow the focus to a specific, well-defined user segment rather than the entire addressable market. Describe them by behavior and need, not just demographics. The tighter the segment, the clearer the signal.

2. **Measure the Sean Ellis Test** — Survey active users: "How would you feel if you could no longer use this product?" If 40% or more answer "Very disappointed," the product likely has PMF for that segment. Below 40%, iterate on the value proposition. Segment the results to find where the signal is strongest.

3. **Analyze Retention Curves** — Plot cohort retention over time. If the curve flattens (asymptotic retention > 0%), users are sticking. If it trends toward zero, the product has not yet achieved PMF regardless of what users say in surveys. Compare retention across segments.

4. **Evaluate Leading Indicators** — Check for organic pull signals: word-of-mouth growth, inbound demand, low CAC relative to LTV, users finding workarounds to access the product, support tickets about feature requests (not complaints), and NPS above 50 among the core segment.

5. **Identify What to Double Down On** — For the segment showing the strongest PMF signals, identify: what specific use case they rely on, what feature they value most, what language they use to describe the product. This becomes the foundation for positioning and growth messaging.

6. **Decide: Iterate or Scale** — If PMF signals are strong, shift resources toward growth (acquisition, onboarding optimization, infrastructure). If signals are weak or inconsistent, continue iterating: adjust the value proposition, change the target segment, or pivot the solution approach.

## Output Format

A structured report containing:

- **Target Segment Definition**: Behavioral description of the primary segment being evaluated
- **Sean Ellis Score**: Percentage of "very disappointed" respondents, segmented by user type
- **Retention Analysis**: Cohort retention curves with commentary on flattening trends
- **Leading Indicator Dashboard**: Organic growth rate, CAC/LTV ratio, NPS, and qualitative pull signals
- **PMF Diagnosis**: Clear verdict — PMF achieved, emerging, or not yet — with supporting evidence
- **Recommended Next Actions**: Specific actions depending on the diagnosis (iterate on X, scale Y, pivot from Z)
