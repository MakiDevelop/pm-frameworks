# Lean Canvas

A one-page business model tool adapted from the Business Model Canvas for lean startup methodology. Created by Ash Maurya, it replaces partner- and resource-focused blocks with problem, solution, key metrics, and unfair advantage — shifting the focus from planning to hypothesis testing. The canvas forces founders and product teams to articulate their biggest assumptions concisely and identify which ones to validate first.

## Steps

1. **Define the Problem and Customer Segments** — List the top 1-3 problems the target customer faces. Identify early adopters (the specific subset of customers who feel the pain most acutely and will try an imperfect solution). Note existing alternatives — how do customers solve this problem today?

2. **Articulate the Unique Value Proposition** — Write a single, clear sentence that explains why the product is different and worth paying attention to. It should be specific, benefit-oriented, and targeted at the early adopters. Avoid jargon and generic claims.

3. **Outline the Solution** — For each top problem, sketch the simplest possible solution feature or approach. Keep it minimal — this is a hypothesis, not a spec. The goal is to identify the minimum feature set that addresses the core problems.

4. **Identify Channels and Revenue Streams** — List the paths to reach early adopters (content, direct sales, partnerships, SEO, communities). Define how the product will make money: pricing model, price point, and expected customer lifetime value. Be specific about the first revenue channel.

5. **Define Key Metrics and Cost Structure** — Choose 3-5 metrics that will tell you whether the business is working (activation rate, retention, revenue per user, etc.). List the major cost drivers: hosting, team, acquisition costs, and fixed expenses. Calculate a rough break-even point.

6. **State the Unfair Advantage** — Identify something that cannot be easily copied or bought by competitors: proprietary data, network effects, domain expertise, community, existing audience, regulatory position, or unique team background. If there is no unfair advantage yet, acknowledge it — this is a risk to address.

## Output Format

A structured report containing:

- **Lean Canvas (One Page)**: All nine blocks filled in concisely:
  - Problem (top 3) + Existing Alternatives
  - Customer Segments + Early Adopters
  - Unique Value Proposition + High-Level Concept
  - Solution (mapped to each problem)
  - Channels
  - Revenue Streams
  - Cost Structure
  - Key Metrics
  - Unfair Advantage
- **Riskiest Assumption**: The single assumption that, if wrong, invalidates the entire model
- **First Experiment**: A concrete, low-cost experiment to test the riskiest assumption within 1-2 weeks
- **Go/No-Go Criteria**: What result from the experiment would signal to proceed vs. pivot
