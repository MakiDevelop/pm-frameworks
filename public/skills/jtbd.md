# Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)

A demand-side innovation framework that focuses on the "job" a customer is trying to accomplish rather than the product itself. Instead of asking "who is our customer?", JTBD asks "what progress is the customer trying to make in a given circumstance?" This reframes competition around outcomes, not features.

## Steps

1. **Define the Job** — Articulate the core job statement in the format: "When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [expected outcome]." Focus on the functional, emotional, and social dimensions of the job.

2. **Map the Job Process** — Break the job into discrete steps the customer goes through: define, locate, prepare, confirm, execute, monitor, modify, conclude. Identify where current solutions fail or create friction.

3. **Identify Hiring and Firing Criteria** — Determine what causes a customer to "hire" a new solution (push from current, pull toward new) and what holds them back (anxiety of new, habit of current). Map these four forces.

4. **Uncover Underserved Outcomes** — For each job step, list the outcomes customers use to measure success (speed, reliability, likelihood of error, etc.). Survey or interview to find which outcomes are important but poorly satisfied.

5. **Prioritize Opportunities** — Score each outcome by importance and satisfaction. High importance + low satisfaction = top opportunity. Use an opportunity score (importance + max(importance - satisfaction, 0)) to rank.

6. **Design Solutions Around the Job** — Generate product or feature concepts that directly address the top underserved outcomes. Validate that each concept meaningfully advances the customer's desired progress.

## Output Format

A structured report containing:

- **Job Statement**: One-sentence description of the core job in [situation / motivation / outcome] format
- **Job Map**: Step-by-step breakdown of the job process with pain points annotated
- **Four Forces Diagram**: Push, pull, anxiety, and habit forces influencing switching behavior
- **Outcome Scores Table**: Each outcome with importance score, satisfaction score, and opportunity score
- **Top Opportunities**: Ranked list of underserved outcomes with recommended solution directions
- **Competitive Landscape**: How current alternatives perform against the top job outcomes
