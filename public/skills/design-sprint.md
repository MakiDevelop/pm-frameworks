# Design Sprint (Google Ventures)

A five-day structured process for answering critical business questions through design, prototyping, and user testing. Developed at Google Ventures, the Design Sprint compresses months of debate into one week by enforcing time-boxed exercises and a bias toward making and testing rather than discussing. It is best suited for high-stakes decisions where the cost of building the wrong thing is significant.

## Steps

1. **Monday — Map and Choose a Target** — Define the long-term goal and list sprint questions (what must be true for this to succeed?). Map the customer journey end-to-end. Conduct expert interviews with stakeholders, engineers, and customer-facing team members. By end of day, the Decider picks a specific target: one customer type and one moment in the journey to focus the sprint on.

2. **Tuesday — Sketch Solutions** — Review existing ideas, competitor approaches, and analogous solutions from other industries (Lightning Demos). Each participant individually sketches detailed solution concepts — not brainstorming as a group. Use the Crazy 8s exercise for rapid ideation, then produce a single refined Solution Sketch per person. Anonymity reduces bias.

3. **Wednesday — Decide and Storyboard** — Review all solution sketches in a structured "art museum" silent critique. Vote on the strongest concepts using dot voting, then the Decider makes the final call. Create a step-by-step storyboard (6-12 panels) of the prototype experience. This storyboard is the blueprint for Thursday's build.

4. **Thursday — Prototype** — Build a realistic-looking facade of the solution. It does not need to work — it needs to look real enough that a test user can react to it authentically. Use tools like Figma, Keynote, or a landing page builder. Assign roles: Makers, Stitcher (continuity), Writer (realistic copy), Asset Collector, Interviewer (prepares for Friday).

5. **Friday — Test with Users** — Conduct five 1-on-1 interviews with target users. Show the prototype, observe their reactions, and ask structured questions. Look for patterns: if 3 out of 5 users struggle with the same thing, that is a signal. Debrief as a team and decide: proceed, iterate, or pivot.

## Output Format

A structured report containing:

- **Sprint Brief**: Long-term goal, sprint questions, and the chosen target (customer + journey moment)
- **Solution Concepts**: Summary of top sketched solutions with key differentiators
- **Decision Log**: Which concept was selected, why, and what was rejected
- **Storyboard**: The 6-12 panel prototype blueprint
- **Prototype**: Link or screenshots of the testable prototype
- **User Test Results**: Findings from each of the five interviews, organized by sprint question
- **Pattern Summary**: Recurring themes across users — what worked, what confused, what excited
- **Next Steps**: Clear recommendation — build, iterate with changes, or rethink the approach
